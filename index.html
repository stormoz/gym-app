How MAG scoring works

The quick version for gymnasts, parents and new judges.

Compulsory levels (1–6): start from 10.0 (+ bonus) +
In the Foundation and Junior levels, every gymnast performs the same set (compulsory) routine on each apparatus, beginning at a Start Value of 10.0. Judges subtract deductions for execution errors (bent arms/legs, separated legs, poor form), missing or altered elements, balance checks, low amplitude and falls. On top of the 10.0, the harder levels add a bonus: +0.5 at Level 4 and +1.0 at Levels 5–6 — lifting the start value to 10.5 and 11.0 — on every apparatus except Vault, which always starts from 10.0. So your score = start value (+ any bonus) − total deductions, and the all-around (AA) is the sum of all six apparatus.
Optional routines — build your own (age rules) +
An optional routine is one the gymnast composes themselves to meet a set of Special Requirements. The score combines a Difficulty score (D) — built from the value of the counted skills and connections — and an Execution score (E) out of 10.0. Final = D + E − any neutral deductions, and missing a Special Requirement or value part typically costs 0.50 each. When you may build your own depends on age: an over-age gymnast can make their own routines from Level 6 and above, while an under-age gymnast competes set routines for longer and can only make their own from Level 9 and above. Below those points, routines are set for you.
The six apparatus & the all-around +
MAG has six apparatus: Floor, Pommel Horse, Rings, Vault, Parallel Bars and Horizontal Bar. In junior squads, the mushroom is used to learn pommel circles before moving onto the horse. A gymnast's all-around (AA) score adds together their best score on each apparatus — this is the figure used for Level Award Test (LAT) benchmarks and most placings.
What is the LAT pass mark? +
The Level Award Test (LAT) is the all-around score a gymnast needs to be considered to have "passed" a level. Widely used ALP benchmarks are 30.00 AA for Levels 3–6, then 38.00 (L7), 39.00 (L8), 40.00 (L9) and 41.00 (L10). Foundation levels (1–2) are generally assessed by the club rather than a fixed national mark.
